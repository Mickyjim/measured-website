How to respond to a narcissist over text

The bait to avoid, why “grey rock” works, and 5 short replies that protect your peace.

Texting with someone who has narcissistic patterns can feel like a trap with no exit: defend yourself and you've “engaged,” stay silent and they escalate. The good news is there's a reliable approach — and it's the opposite of what your adrenaline wants you to do.

The one rule: don't JADE

JADE = Justify, Argue, Defend, Explain. Every one of those gives a reaction to feed on and a new thread to pull. The more reasonable and detailed your reply, the more material they have. With high-conflict people, your calm, boring brevity is the most powerful thing you've got.

Grey rock: be unreactive on purpose

“Grey rock” means becoming as interesting as a grey rock — short, neutral, fact-only. Not cold or cruel, just flat. No emotion to mirror, no hook to grab. Over time, an unreactive target is far less rewarding to provoke.

5 grey-rock replies

Logistics onlyNoted. I'll be there at 5.
Decline the baitI'm not going to get into that. Let me know about the schedule when you can.
Acknowledge without engagingOkay. Thanks for letting me know.
Redirect to the pointThe only thing I need to sort out is [logistic]. Can we keep it to that?
End the threadI think we've covered it. Talk soon.

Why these work

Each reply does the minimum: it keeps things moving, gives no emotional reaction, and quietly refuses the argument. You're not winning a debate — you're declining to play. That's how you protect your energy without escalating.

A note. “Narcissist” gets used loosely; only a professional can diagnose anyone. But the tactics above work for any high-conflict, draining communicator. If the relationship involves control, intimidation, or fear, that's beyond texting strategy — please reach out to someone you trust or a professional, and prioritise your safety.
