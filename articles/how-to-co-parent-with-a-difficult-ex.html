How to co-parent with a difficult ex over text

Keep it about the kids, not the fight — the BIFF method and 6 calm, on-the-record replies.

Co-parenting texts are their own special kind of hard. A simple message about pickup time arrives wrapped in a dig, and suddenly you're being pulled back into the relationship you left. The goal isn't to win those exchanges — it's to stay out of them while keeping things moving for your kids.

The method: BIFF

Brief. Informative. Friendly. Firm. Brief so there's little to argue with. Informative so the logistics are clear. Friendly (not warm — just civil) so it can't be quoted as hostile. Firm so your boundary holds. Reply to the business in their message and let the bait sit there, unanswered.

Write like a judge might read it

Assume every message could be read aloud in a mediation or courtroom one day. That single mental filter strips out the sarcasm, the score-keeping, and the late-night paragraphs — and it's exactly the tone that de-escalates a high-conflict co-parent anyway.

6 calm replies

Confirm logistics, ignore the digThanks. Pickup is 5pm Friday as agreed. See you then.
Stay on the kidsFor Sam's appointment: it's Tuesday at 3. I can take him if that works for you.
Decline the relationship talkI'm going to keep our messages to the kids and scheduling. For Mia's school form, the deadline is Friday.
Offer options, not argumentsI can do either Saturday morning or Sunday afternoon for the swap. Which works better?
Hold a boundary, brieflyThat's not something I'll discuss over text. The handover plan still stands.
Close cleanlySounds good — see you Friday.

Why these work

Each reply answers the real question and quietly refuses the fight. You're modelling the calm your kids need, protecting yourself with a clean record, and starving the conflict of oxygen. Boring, in co-parenting, is a win.

Quick answers

How do you respond to a difficult co-parent over text?
Use BIFF — Brief, Informative, Friendly, Firm. Stick to logistics and the kids, ignore the bait, and write as if a judge could read it: “Thanks. Pickup is 5pm Friday as agreed.”
Should you keep co-parenting messages on the record?
Yes — keep it in writing and factual. It reduces conflict, keeps the focus on the children, and gives you a clear record if needed.
