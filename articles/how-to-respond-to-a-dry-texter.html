How to respond to a dry texter

One-word replies, zero questions back — what it means, and how to stop carrying the whole conversation.

“Haha yeah.” “Nice.” “For sure.” You send a thoughtful message and get back three words and no question. Dry texting is exhausting because it quietly turns you into the entertainment director of a conversation the other person isn't really in. Here's how to stop doing that.

What dry texting usually means

Two possibilities, and they look identical at first: (1) they're just a low-effort or awkward texter who's warm in person, or (2) they're keeping you at arm's length. You can't tell from the texts alone — you tell from effort overall. Do they make plans, call, show up? That's the real signal. Texting style is noise; effort is the data.

The trap to avoid

Over-investing to compensate. Sending paragraphs to a “k,” asking question after question, working twice as hard to keep it alive. That trains them that they can coast while you carry it. Stop rowing for two.

4 ways to respond

Match their energy(Reply with the same effort they give. Short gets short. Let the silence be a little awkward.)
Ask something un-one-word-ableWhat's been the best part of your week so far? (Open questions are harder to answer dryly.)
Move it off textI'm not much of a texter either tbh — want to grab a coffee and actually catch up?
Name it lightlyYou're killing me with these one-word replies 😄 give me something to work with!

Why these work

Each one stops you from doing all the work and quietly hands the effort back. If they step up — great, the dryness was just their texting style. If they keep coasting, you've got your answer without a single dramatic conversation. Either way, you've stopped over-functioning for someone who isn't matching you.

Quick answers

How do you respond to a dry texter?
Stop carrying it. Match their energy, ask open questions that can't be answered in one word, or move it off text: “Want to just grab a coffee and catch up?”
Does dry texting mean they're not interested?
Not always — some people are low-effort texters but warm in person. Watch their effort overall (plans, calls, showing up), not just their texting style.
