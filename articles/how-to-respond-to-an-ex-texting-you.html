How to respond to an ex texting you

The name lights up the screen and your stomach drops — here's how to reply on purpose, not on impulse.

An ex's text can undo a week of progress in two seconds. Before you fire back something you'll re-read fifty times, the move is to slow down and answer one question first: what do you actually want here? The right reply depends entirely on that — not on what they want.

Decide your goal before you reply

Are you after closure? Open to friendship? Wanting clean distance? Done entirely? There's no wrong answer — but answering from adrenaline (or from who you were when you were together) is how people get pulled back in. Name your goal, then pick the reply that protects it.

The trap to avoid

Treating their text as a question you're obligated to answer fully. You're not. You don't owe an explanation, a debate, or a deep-dive into what went wrong. A short reply — or none — is a complete sentence.

5 replies for whatever you want

If you want distanceHey. I hope you're well. I'm focused on moving forward right now, so I'm going to keep my distance. Take care.
If you're open but cautiousGood to hear from you. I'm doing well. What's on your mind?
If you want closure on one thingI'm happy to answer that. After this, though, I think it's healthier for me to leave it here.
If it's logistics (returning things, etc.)Sure — I can drop your things off Saturday, or you're welcome to collect them. Let me know which is easier.
If you want no contact(No reply is a valid answer. If you do reply:) I'd prefer we don't keep in touch. I wish you well.

Why these work

Each one comes from your decision, kindly delivered — not from the old reflex to manage their feelings or keep the door cracked “just in case.” You can be gracious and still hold your ground. The calm of a reply you chose on purpose is worth far more than the relief of a reply you fired off on impulse.

Quick answers

How do you respond to an ex texting you?
Decide what you want first — closure, friendship, space, or no contact — then reply from that, not from adrenaline. For distance: “I'm focused on moving forward, so I'm going to keep my distance. Take care.”
Should you reply to an ex's text?
Only if it serves you. You're never obligated. A brief, kind reply — or no reply at all — is a valid choice.
