How to apologize over text (that actually lands)

The 4-part formula, the words that ruin it, and 5 examples to adapt.

Most apology texts fail for the same reason: they're really self-defence in disguise. “I'm sorry, but…” isn't an apology — it's an argument with a polite hat on. A real one is simple, and it has four parts.

The formula: name it, own the impact, say sorry, repair

  • Name what you did — specifically, not “for everything.”
  • Acknowledge the impact — show you get how it landed.
  • Say sorry plainly — no “if,” no “but.”
  • Say what you'll do differently — small and concrete.

The words that ruin it

“I'm sorry you feel…” (blames their feelings). “I'm sorry but…” (cancels the apology). “I'm sorry if…” (pretends you're not sure you did anything). Cut all three. The moment a “but” shows up, the other person stops hearing the apology and starts defending themselves.

5 apologies that land

After snappingI snapped at you last night and made you feel dismissed. That wasn't fair, and I'm sorry. I'm going to take a breath before I react next time.
After being late / flakyI left you waiting and that was disrespectful of your time. I'm sorry. I'll set a reminder so it doesn't happen again.
After crossing a lineWhat I said went too far. You didn't deserve that, and I'm genuinely sorry. I'd like to do better.
When you need a moment firstI've been thinking about earlier. I was defensive and I shut you down, and I'm sorry — you were trying to tell me something real.
Short and sincereI was wrong and I'm sorry. No excuses. Thank you for being patient with me.

Why these work

Each one keeps the focus on your action and their experience — never on justifying yourself. That's the whole secret: a good apology asks for nothing back. No demand to be forgiven, no list of their faults. Just ownership, plainly stated. That's what actually repairs trust.

Quick answers

How do you apologize over text?
Name what you did, acknowledge the impact, say sorry plainly, and say what you'll change — no “but.” “I snapped and made you feel dismissed. That wasn't fair and I'm sorry.”
What makes an apology fake?
Shifting blame or adding conditions: “sorry you feel that way,” “sorry but you started it,” “sorry if I upset you.” Own the action and impact without “if” or “but.”
